Code of Conduct

Read the code of conduct carefully!

Attention all neurotechies of the upcoming Neuroverse coding competition! We want to inform you that the prelims will be held on 30 March, 2023 at 1:00 pm in the computer science department. The prelims will be a 30 minute multiple-choice question test designed to evaluate your knowledge and skills in coding. Only those participants who achieve the qualifying marks in the prelims will be allowed to proceed to the final coding competition on 1 April, 2023. The final competition will be a two-hour long competitive coding round where you will be expected to showcase your abilities to solve interesting problems. Please make sure to arrive at the venue on time and adhere to the rules and regulations set forth by the organizing committee. It is important that all participants maintain a respectful and professional demeanor towards their fellow competitors and judges throughout the competition. The judges' decision will be final and binding, and no appeals will be entertained. We expect all participants to compete with honesty, integrity, and sportsmanship, and any violation of the code of conduct may lead to disqualification from the competition. We wish you the best of luck in your endeavors and look forward to seeing your skills and abilities in action!
